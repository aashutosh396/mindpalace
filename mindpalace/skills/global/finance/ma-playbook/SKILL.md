# M&A Playbook

Frameworks for both sides: acquiring and being acquired.

## When you're acquiring

**Strategic rationale first:** buy vs build (can you build faster/cheaper? then don't acquire); acqui-hire vs product vs market (what are you really buying — talent, tech, customers?); integration complexity.

**Due-diligence checklist (domain → red flag):** Financial (revenue quality, concentration) → >30% revenue from 1 customer · Technical (code quality, fit) → monolith with no tests · Legal (IP, litigation) → key IP owned by individuals · People (key-person risk, retention) → founders with no lockup/earnout · Market (position, threats) → declining share · Customers (churn, NPS, terms) → high churn, short contracts.

**Valuation (illustrative — verify against current comps):** revenue multiple (industry/growth/NRR-dependent), comparable transactions (most defensible anchor), DCF (profitable companies only), acqui-hire (~$1–3M/engineer in hot markets). Cross-check ≥2 current sources before anchoring a price.

## When you're being acquired

**Readiness signals:** inbound strategic interest, market consolidation, fundraising harder than operating, founder ready to transition.
**Prep (6–12 months out):** clean up (audit) financials, document IP/contracts, reduce customer concentration, lock up key employees, build the data room, engage an M&A advisor.
**Negotiation watch-points:** valuation (earnout traps with unreachable targets) · earnout (milestone definitions, measurement period) · lockup (duration/conditions) · reps & warranties (escrow vs indemnification cap) · employee retention (who gets offers, at what terms). Leverage = competing offers and key-person dependencies.

## Red flags (both sides)

No clear rationale beyond "good deal" · culture clash seen in DD and ignored · key people not locked before close · no integration plan ("we'll figure it out") · valuation based on projections not actuals.

## Verification loop (before any LOI/signature)

Frame the deal here, then verify (don't duplicate): legal terms → run LOI/term sheet + definitive docs through a contract/term-sheet risk review; any red finding → outside counsel. Data diligence → audit the target's data estate for training-data rights and data-asset value; undocumented consent provenance is a price-reduction or walk-away item. Valuation math → CFO tools for the quantitative model. Loop findings back into the negotiation-points table before the next counter.
