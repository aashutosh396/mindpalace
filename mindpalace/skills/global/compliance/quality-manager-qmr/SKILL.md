QMS accountability, management review leadership, regulatory oversight per ISO 13485 Clause 5.5.2.

## QMR responsibilities (Clause 5.5.2)
Monitor QMS effectiveness · report performance to top management · promote regulatory/quality awareness · liaise with regulators + Notified Bodies. Accountable for: quality policy (annual, →CEO/Board), objectives (quarterly), QMS performance (monthly), regulatory compliance (quarterly), audit program, CAPA oversight (monthly).

## Management review (Clause 5.6)
Schedule ≥ annually (typically quarterly/semi-annual), notify attendees 2wk prior, distribute agenda + input package 1wk prior.
**Required inputs (5.6.2):** audit results · customer feedback (complaints, satisfaction, returns) · process performance + product conformity · CAPA status · previous review actions · changes affecting QMS (regulatory, organizational) · improvement recommendations.
**Required outputs (5.6.3):** QMS/process improvement decisions (action items with owners + dates) · product improvement · resource needs.
*Validation: all required inputs reviewed; decisions documented with owners and due dates.*

## Quality KPI framework
Set KPIs per objective using SMART, with owner + target + data source + escalation threshold.
| KPI | Target | Calc |
|---|---|---|
| First Pass Yield | >95% | passed first time / total ×100 |
| Nonconformance rate | <1% | NC / total ×100 |
| CAPA closure rate | >90% | on-time / due ×100 |
| CAPA effectiveness | >85% | effective / verified ×100 |
| Finding closure | >90% | on-time / due ×100 |
| Repeat finding rate | <10% | repeats / total ×100 |
| Complaint rate | <0.1% | complaints / units sold ×100 |
| Satisfaction | >4.0/5.0 | survey avg |
Review frequency by type: safety/compliance daily · production quality weekly · customer quality monthly · strategic quality quarterly.
Performance response: >110% exceeding (raise target) · 100-110% meeting · 90-100% approaching (monitor) · 80-90% below (improvement plan) · <80% critical (immediate intervention).

## Quality objectives (Clause 5.4.1)
Annual: review prior achievement → analyze trends/gaps → align to strategy → draft measurable targets → validate resources → executive approval → communicate org-wide. Each objective: statement, policy linkage, target, baseline, owner, due date, success criteria, measurement method, reporting frequency. *Validation: each is measurable with owner, target, timeline.*

## Quality culture (annual assessment)
Survey dimensions: leadership commitment, quality ownership, communication, continuous improvement, training/competence, problem-solving. *Validation: response rate >60%; action plan addresses bottom 3 scores.* Gap→action: low leadership visibility→gemba walks · inadequate training→competency program · poor communication→huddles · low reporting→anonymous no-blame system · no recognition→quality award program.

## Regulatory oversight
Multi-jurisdiction matrix: EU MDR 2017/745 (CE + Notified Body) · USA 21 CFR 820 QMSR (incorporates ISO 13485:2016 by reference, effective 2026-02-02; formerly QSR) · ISO 13485 (surveillance audits) · Germany MPG/MPDG. Maintain requirement register, assess change impact monthly, update processes within 90 days, maintain inspection-readiness checklist (document control, training, CAPA, complaints, calibration, supplier files, management review, audits).
**Decision discipline:** this is decision support, not a compliance determination — final regulatory calls are the QMR's, signed by the named owner; verify current 21 CFR 820 / ISO 13485:2016 text at fda.gov before citing.

## Escalation
Regulatory violation → executive immediately. Safety issue → safety team. Multi-department → executive. Else → department level.
