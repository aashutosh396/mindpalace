# Opportunity Solution Tree

An Opportunity Solution Tree (OST, Teresa Torres) is a visual discovery framework connecting a business outcome to customer opportunities and potential solutions. It prevents jumping straight to solutions by ensuring every feature idea traces back to a customer need and a measurable outcome.

## When to use

- Organizing learning during continuous product discovery
- Prioritizing which opportunities to pursue
- Communicating product strategy to stakeholders
- When you have too many feature ideas and need structure
- Connecting user research insights to action

## When NOT to use

- Scoring/ranking a flat list of known candidates → use a prioritization framework
- Framing one specific problem for a team → problem statement
- Ready to test a single assumption → hypothesis, then experiment design
- The outcome to drive isn't agreed yet → set it first (OKRs); a tree without an agreed outcome just decorates opinions

## How

1. **Define the desired outcome.** Measurable, product-influenceable, quantitative when possible (e.g. "Increase 30-day retention from 40% to 55%").
2. **Identify opportunity areas.** 3-5 branches — customer problems/needs/desires from the customer's perspective, NOT solutions.
3. **Add supporting evidence.** Per opportunity: research quotes, behavioral data, support tickets, market trends. Strong opportunities cite multiple sources.
4. **Brainstorm solutions.** 2-4 per opportunity; don't self-censor; keep specific enough to evaluate.
5. **Define assumption tests.** For each promising solution, name the riskiest assumption and a lightweight experiment to test it.
6. **Prioritize the tree.** Mark which opportunity/solution to pursue first based on impact, confidence, effort. Living document.
7. **Visualize.** Tree diagram: outcome on top → opportunities → solutions → experiments at the leaves.

## Output sections

Desired Outcome; Visual Tree; Opportunity Branches; Prioritization; Experiments Backlog; Learning Log; Next Steps.

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Outcome measurable and within team influence
- [ ] Opportunities customer-centric (needs, not features)
- [ ] Each opportunity has documented evidence
- [ ] Multiple solutions per opportunity (no jumping to one)
- [ ] Assumptions explicit with experiments designed
- [ ] Clear which branch to explore first
