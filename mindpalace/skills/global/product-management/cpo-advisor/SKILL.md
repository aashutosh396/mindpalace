# CPO Advisor

Strategic product leadership: decisions that determine what gets built, why, and by whom. Reasoning: first principles — decompose to fundamental user needs, question every assumption, rebuild from validated evidence not inherited roadmaps.

## The CPO owns three things (everything else is delegation)

**Portfolio** (which products exist, get investment, get killed) · **Vision** (where the product goes in 3–5 years and why customers care) · **Org** (structure that can execute) · plus **PMF** (measure/achieve/not lose) and **Metrics** (north-star hierarchy, board reporting).

## Diagnostic questions

Portfolio: which product is the dog — killing it or lying to yourself? If you cut 30% tomorrow, what stays? Combined D30 retention trending up? PMF: retention curve for best cohort? % users "very disappointed" if it disappeared? Organic growth without pushing? Org: can every PM articulate the north star? Last time the trio did user interviews together? Strategy: if only one thing this quarter, what and why? Moat in 12 months / 3 years? Riskiest assumption in current strategy?

## Product metrics hierarchy

North Star (1, owned by CPO — measures customer value delivered, not revenue; every team can influence) → leading indicators (3–5, owned by PMs) → lagging (revenue, churn, NPS). Good north stars: B2B SaaS = weekly active accounts using core feature · consumer = D30 retained · marketplace = successful transactions/week · PLG = accounts reaching aha within 14 days.

## Investment postures (every product gets one)

**Invest** (high growth, strong/growing retention → full team, aggressive roadmap) · **Maintain** (stable revenue, slow growth, good margins → bug fixes only, milk it) · **Kill** (declining, flat/negative margins, no recovery → sunset date + migration plan). "Wait and see" is a decision to lose share.

## Red flags

Portfolio: "question marks" for 2+ quarters with no decision; capacity on highest-revenue product while highest-growth is understaffed; >30% time on declining-revenue products. PMF: must convince users to keep using it; support is "how do I do X" not "I want X to also do Y"; D30 below 20% (consumer)/40% (B2B), not improving. Org: waterfall in agile clothing; platform team 6-week queue; CPO hasn't talked to a customer in 30+ days. Metrics: north star up while retention down (wrong metric); roadmap built from sales requests, not user behavior.

## Output artifacts

PMF scorecard (retention/engagement/satisfaction/growth) · prioritized backlog with scoring framework · portfolio map with invest/maintain/kill · org proposal with team topology + PM ratios · product board section (metrics + roadmap + risks).
