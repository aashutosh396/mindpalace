# Competitive Intelligence

Systematic tracking — intelligence that drives decisions, not obsession.

## 5-layer system

### Layer 1 — Identification
Direct (same ICP/problem/price), indirect (same budget, different solution — incl. "do nothing" and "build in-house"), future (well-funded adjacent startups, incumbents with roadmap overlap). 2×2 threat matrix (ICP × problem): same/same = direct threat, same problem/diff ICP = adjacent watch, diff problem/same ICP = displacement risk. Update quarterly — who moved?

### Layer 2 — Tracking dimensions (per competitor)
Product moves (changelog, G2/Capterra, social — monthly) · pricing (page, sales intel — triggered) · funding (Crunchbase, TC — triggered) · hiring (LinkedIn jobs — monthly) · partnerships (PR — triggered) · customer wins (case studies — monthly) · customer losses (win/loss interviews — ongoing) · messaging shifts (homepage, ad libraries — quarterly).

### Layer 3 — Analysis
SWOT per competitor · positioning map (pick 2 axes that matter to buyers and show YOUR differentiation) · feature gap analysis (you vs each competitor → advantage / gap / moat).

### Layer 4 — Outputs
Sales (CRO): one-page battlecards for pre-call prep · Marketing (CMO): positioning update (message shifts, claims to start/stop) · Product (CPO): feature-gap summary · CEO/Board: monthly 1-page summary (who moved, what it means, response).

### Layer 5 — Cadence
Monthly: review tier-1 competitors, update battlecards, publish summary. Triggered: funding (assess within 48h), major feature (response within 1 week), poached customer (win/loss within 2 weeks), pricing change (respond within 1 week). Quarterly: full landscape review, update positioning map, refresh tracking list.

## Win/loss analysis (highest-signal data)

Interview: every lost deal >$50K ACV, every churn >6mo tenure, every competitive win. NOT the AE who worked the deal (CS, product, or external researcher). Questions: walk me through your evaluation → who else considered → top 3 criteria → where did we fall short → deciding factor → what would have changed your decision. Aggregate monthly: rank win/loss reasons, competitor win rates by segment, patterns over time.

## The balance

Over-tracking signs: roadmap driven by "they shipped X," morale drops when competitors raise, shipping features you don't believe in. Under-tracking signs: AEs blindsided, prospects know more than your team, missed a major launch. Right posture: know them well enough to win; don't let them set your agenda; roadmap led by customer problems, informed by competitive gaps.

## Red flags

Competitor win rate >50% in your core segment (positioning problem, not sales) · same objection from 5+ deals ("competitor has X" — real gap) · competitor hired 10 engineers in your domain · competitor raised >$20M targeting your ICP (12-month runway to compete hard) · prospects evaluate you to justify a competitor decision (you're the checkbox).

Keep all intel in one source of truth (Notion/Confluence/CRM) — never Slack-only.
