# Competitive Analysis

Structured insight into the competitive landscape: where you stand relative to alternatives and where to differentiate. Focus on actionable insights that inform strategy, not an exhaustive catalog of every competitor.

## When to use

- Before entering a new market or launching a product
- Planning differentiation for an existing product
- During quarterly/annual strategic planning
- Evaluating build vs. buy
- After losing deals, to understand positioning
- Onboarding team members to market context

## When NOT to use

- You need market size, not positioning → market sizing
- Stress-testing differentiation across the whole business model → lean canvas
- Understanding why customers switch → JTBD canvas
- Landscape already mapped and you need the problem framed → problem statement

## How

1. **Define scope.** Feature area, overall positioning, or pricing. Pick 3-5 competitors: direct (same solution), indirect (different solution, same problem), potential disruptors.
2. **Gather intelligence.** Public sources: websites, pricing pages, G2/Capterra, press, job postings, testimonials. Mark verified vs. inferred.
3. **Build a feature matrix.** Compare capabilities that matter to your target customers; consistent ratings (Full/Partial/None/Unknown).
4. **Analyze positioning.** 2×2 on market-relevant dimensions (price vs features, ease vs power, SMB vs enterprise). Identify white space.
5. **Assess strengths & weaknesses.** Genuine strengths and real weaknesses per competitor — respect drives better strategy.
6. **Identify strategic implications.** Where to compete head-on, where to differentiate, messaging to emphasize, gaps = opportunities.
7. **Note confidence levels.** Verified vs. inferred; be honest about uncertainty.

## Output sections

Overview; Market Context; Competitors Analyzed; Feature Comparison Matrix; Pricing Comparison; Positioning Map; Competitor Deep Dives; Competitive Gaps & Opportunities; Strategic Recommendations; Sources & Confidence; Next Steps.

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Scope clear (market, segment, use case)
- [ ] 3-5 competitors incl. direct + indirect
- [ ] Feature comparison customer-relevant
- [ ] Positioning dimensions meaningful and differentiated
- [ ] Strengths acknowledge where competitors excel
- [ ] Recommendations specific; sources/confidence documented
