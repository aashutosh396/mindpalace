# Investigation Toolkit

Practical truth-finding: tracing sources, decomposing claims, evaluating evidence, generating rival hypotheses, triangulating across independent sources. Diagnose what's needed and route.

## Which tool fits

| You need to... | Tool |
|---|---|
| Trace a claim to its origin, test transmission | Source Trace |
| Break a claim into verifiable parts | Claim Decomposition |
| Evaluate quality/strength/completeness of evidence | Evidence Audit |
| Generate the best alternative explanations | Counter-Hypothesis |
| Verify across genuinely independent sources | Triangulation |

When unclear → Claim Decomposition (surfaces what you're actually trying to verify, then route further). Tools can be chained on the same claim.

## Source Trace

Trace the claim to its earliest source: who said it, in what context, with what evidence. Map how it changed propagating — paraphrase, scope shift, dropped caveats. Test the source's credibility and incentives.
**Output:** Origin, transmission map with distortion points, source credibility, verdict on whether the current claim is faithful to the original.

## Claim Decomposition

Surface the hidden architecture: what sub-claims are inside, which are independently verifiable vs assumed, which carry the most logical load. "The market is shifting toward X" typically hides 5–8 claims — direction, pace, magnitude, causation, durability, relevance — each separately falsifiable.
**Output:** Full sub-claim decomposition, each classified (verified/checkable/uncheckable/assumed), highest-load sub-claims, verification priorities.

## Evidence Audit

Structured quality assessment: evidence type (RCT vs observational vs anecdote vs expert opinion), sample quality, methodology, conflicts of interest, and what is notably absent. Locate where current evidence sits on the hierarchy and what stronger evidence looks like.
**Output:** Evidence inventory, type/quality classification, notable absences, conflict-of-interest flags, overall verdict.

## Counter-Hypothesis

Systematically generate rival explanations that fit the evidence equally well or better. For each, identify what evidence would distinguish it from the original. Name the decisive test that most cleanly discriminates between explanations.
**Output:** Restated claim, 3–5 rival hypotheses with supporting evidence, distinguishing evidence, and the decisive test.

## Triangulation

Collect candidate sources; classify by independence (many sources tracing to one original are amplification, not independence). Assess whether truly independent sources converge (more reliable) or diverge (contested/uncertain).
**Output:** Source list, independence classification, convergence/divergence assessment, reliability verdict.
