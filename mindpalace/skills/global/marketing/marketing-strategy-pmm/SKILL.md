Product marketing patterns: positioning, GTM, competitive intelligence, launches, enablement, market entry.

## ICP definition
1. Analyze top 20% customers by LTV. 2. Common firmographics (size, industry, revenue, geo, funding). 3. Technographics (tools, maturity). 4. Psychographics (pain, motivation, risk tolerance). 5. Define 3-5 personas (economic, technical, user/champion). 6. Validate vs sales-cycle + churn. 7. Score prospects A/B/C/D. **Validation:** A-fit = lowest churn, fastest close.
ICP check: 5+ paying customers match · fastest cycles · highest LTV · churn <5% · strong engagement · willing to do case studies.

## Positioning (April Dunford)
1. List competitive alternatives (direct, adjacent, status quo). 2. Isolate unique attributes. 3. Map attributes → customer value. 4. Define best-fit customers. 5. Choose market category (head-to-head / niche / new). 6. Layer relevant trends. 7. Test with 10+ interviews. **Validation:** 7+ customers describe value unprompted.

**Statement template:** FOR [customer] WHO [need], THE [product] IS A [category] THAT [benefit], UNLIKE [alternative], OUR PRODUCT [differentiation].
**Value prop:** `[Product] helps [Customer] [achieve goal] by [unique approach]`.
**Messaging hierarchy:** Headline (5-7 words) → Subhead (1 sentence) → Benefits (3-4) → Features (evidence) → Proof (social).

## Competitive intelligence
Tier 1 (direct) / 2 (adjacent) / 3 (status quo). Sign up for competitor products, monitor pricing/messaging, mine sales-call recordings + G2/Capterra reviews + job postings (roadmap signals). Update battlecards monthly. **Validation:** sales uses battlecards in 80%+ competitive deals.
**Battlecard:** overview · positioning (they-say vs reality) · strengths · weaknesses · our advantages (+evidence) · when-we-win · when-we-lose · talk track (objection→response).
**Win/loss (monthly):** win rate by competitor, top win/loss reasons, action items for product/sales/marketing.

## Launches (by tier)
| Tier | Scope | Prep | Budget |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | New product/major feature | 6-8 wk | $50-100k |
| 2 | Significant feature/integration | 3-4 wk | $10-25k |
| 3 | Small improvement | 1 wk | <$5k |

Tier-1 flow: kickoff (Product/Mktg/Sales/CS) → goals ($ pipeline, MQLs, press) → positioning → enablement (deck/demo/battlecard) → campaign assets → train sales+CS → launch day → 30-day optimize. **Validation:** pipeline on track by week 2.
Launch day: press release · email · social · paid at full budget · sales outbound · in-app notification · metrics every 2h.

## Sales enablement
Deck (15-20 slides, visual-first) · one-pagers · demo script (30-45min: intro 2 / discovery 5 / demo 20 / Q&A 10 / next steps 3) · email templates · ROI calculator · monthly calls + quarterly training. **Validation:** used in 80%+ opportunities.

## International expansion
Validate demand → localize (site/pricing/legal) → sales coverage → cultural messaging → local partnerships → localized campaigns → monitor CAC by market. **Validation:** 3+ paying customers in first 90 days. Localization: professional translation, local currency/pricing/phone/address, GDPR/PIPEDA, local payment methods, in-hours coverage, local references.

## Proactive flags
No documented positioning · messaging differs across channels · no ICP defined · competitor repositioning detected.
