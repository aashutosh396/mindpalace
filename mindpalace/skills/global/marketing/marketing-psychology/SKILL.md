# Marketing Psychology

Identify which principles apply to a specific challenge and show how to use them — don't just name-drop biases.

## Three modes
- **Diagnose** — analyze a page/flow/campaign through a behavioral lens; identify which principles are violated or underutilized.
- **Apply** — recommend 3-5 principles for an asset with concrete implementation.
- **Reference** — explain a model + marketing application + before/after example.

## Most-used models

**Conversion:**
- Loss Aversion — losses hurt 2x more than gains; frame benefits as what they'll miss.
- Anchoring — first number seen sets expectations; show higher price first.
- Social Proof — show customer count, testimonials, logos.
- Scarcity — only if real; fake urgency backfires.
- Paradox of Choice — too many options = no decision; limit to 3 tiers.

**Pricing:**
- Charm Pricing — $49 reads meaningfully cheaper than $50 (left-digit effect).
- Decoy Effect — add a dominated option to make your target tier obvious.
- Rule of 100 — under $100 show % discount; over $100 show $ discount.

**Copy/messaging:**
- Reciprocity — give value first (free tool/guide/audit).
- Endowment Effect — let people "own" before paying (trial, saved progress).
- Framing — "95% uptime" vs "down 18 days/year"; choose deliberately.

## Situation → models
| Situation | Apply |
|---|---|
| Landing page not converting | Loss Aversion, Social Proof, Anchoring, Hick's Law |
| Pricing page | Charm Pricing, Decoy, Good-Better-Best, Anchoring |
| Email engagement | Reciprocity, Zeigarnik, Goal-Gradient, Commitment |
| Reducing churn | Endowment, Sunk Cost, Switching Costs, Status-Quo Bias |
| Onboarding activation | IKEA Effect, Goal-Gradient, Fogg Model, Default Effect |
| Referral program | Reciprocity, Social Proof, Network Effects, Unity |

## Diagnostic questions
1. Desired behavior? (click/buy/share/return)
2. Current friction? (too many choices / unclear value / no urgency)
3. Emotional state? (excited/skeptical/confused/impatient)
4. Context? (first visit / returning / comparing)
5. Risk tolerance? (high-stakes B2B vs low-stakes consumer impulse)

## Proactive flags
- No social proof on landing page; pricing page shows all features equally (no anchoring/decoy); weak CTA language ("Submit" vs "Start my free trial"); too many form fields (Hick's Law); no urgency element when legitimate scarcity exists.
