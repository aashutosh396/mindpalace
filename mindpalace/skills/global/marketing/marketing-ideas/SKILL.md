# SaaS Marketing Ideas

A library of ~139 proven marketing ideas. Find the right strategies for the user's situation, stage, and resources. Curate ruthlessly — never dump the whole list.

## Process
1. Clarify product, audience, current stage (if unclear).
2. Suggest 3-5 most relevant ideas for their context.
3. Detail implementation for the chosen ones.
4. Respect their resources (time, budget, team).

## Idea categories (quick reference)
Content & SEO (programmatic SEO, glossary marketing, repurposing) · Competitor (comparison pages, jiu-jitsu) · Free Tools (calculators, generators, extensions) · Paid Ads (LinkedIn, Google, retargeting, podcast) · Social & Community (Reddit, short-form video) · Email (founder emails, onboarding, win-back) · Partnerships (affiliate, integration, newsletter swaps) · Events (webinars, speaking, summits) · PR & Media · Launches (Product Hunt, LTDs, giveaways) · Product-Led (viral loops, powered-by, free migrations) · Content Formats (podcasts, courses, year wraps) · Unconventional (awards, challenges, guerrilla) · Platforms (marketplaces, review sites, YouTube) · International · Developer (DevRel, certifications) · Audience-Specific (referrals, podcast tours).

## By stage
- **Pre-launch**: waitlist referrals, early-access pricing, Product Hunt prep.
- **Early**: content & SEO, community, founder-led sales.
- **Growth**: paid acquisition, partnerships, events.
- **Scale**: brand campaigns, international, media acquisitions.

## By budget
Free: content/SEO, community, social, comment marketing. Low: targeted ads, sponsorships, free tools. Medium: events, partnerships, PR. High: acquisitions, conferences, brand.

## By timeline
Quick wins: ads, email, social. Medium: content, SEO, community. Long: brand, thought leadership, platform effects.

## Top ideas by use case
Leads fast: Google Ads, LinkedIn Ads, engineering-as-marketing. Authority: speaking, books, podcasts. Low budget: easy keyword ranking, Reddit, comment marketing. PLG: viral loops, powered-by, in-app upsells. Enterprise: investor marketing, expert networks, conference sponsorship.

## Per-idea output
Idea name (one line) · why it fits their situation · how to start (2-3 steps) · expected outcome · resources needed (time/budget/skills). Always indicate effort + timeline. Tag 🟢 proven for stage / 🟡 worth testing / 🔴 high-variance.

## Proactive flags
Pre-revenue but asking about paid ads → redirect to zero-budget tactics until PMF. "Need more leads" without timeline/budget → clarify first. Copying a competitor's whole playbook → suggest differentiated angles on their blind spots. No owned audience → push list-building first. 5+ channels with team of 1-2 → flag dilution; focus on 1-2.
