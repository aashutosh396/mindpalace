# CMO Review — Six Forcing Questions

Narrative-first: pressure-test positioning before debating tactics. Run before launching a campaign, changing positioning/tagline/category, putting >10% of budget into a new channel, or a major PR moment — or when pipeline contribution is declining.

## The six questions

1. **ICP (one real person).** Name one real person: company, title, daily work, what they hate. Persona ≠ ICP; ICP is real. Can't name one → it isn't sharp enough.
2. **JTBD.** What job is the customer hiring this product to do, and what's the alternative they use today? One sentence the customer would say. "We use spreadsheets" and "we don't" are valid alternatives.
3. **Positioning statement (one sentence).** "For [ICP] who needs [job], we are [category] that [differentiator] unlike [alternative]." This is the headline; everything cascades. Doesn't fit in one sentence → not positioning yet.
4. **Distribution channel.** Where does the customer first hear your name, and is it inbound or outbound at this stage? Name the channel, intent, path to first contact; pick a primary motion (PLG / sales-led / content-led / partnership-led).
5. **CAC payback.** Per channel: CAC, payback in months, improving? Payback >18 months isn't a channel — it's a hobby.
6. **Brand defensibility.** If a well-funded competitor copies your messaging tomorrow, what's still yours? Category position, founder-market fit, customer love, or distribution lock — name one.

## Output

Verdict 🟢 (sharp story, sound channel mix) / 🟡 (sharpen positioning before scaling) / 🔴 (positioning broken — do not spend). Capture the one-sentence positioning statement, named ICP + JTBD, primary/secondary channels with CAC and payback, and 3 concrete next steps. Route pipeline-contribution checks to CRO, product↔positioning alignment to CPO.
