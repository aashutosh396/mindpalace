# Competitor & Alternative Pages

Build pages that rank for competitive search terms, provide genuine value to evaluators, and position your product effectively.

## Initial assessment
Your product (value prop, differentiators, ICP, pricing, honest weaknesses) · competitive landscape (direct/indirect, positioning, search volume) · goals (SEO, sales enablement, switcher conversion, positioning).

## Core principles
1. **Honesty builds trust** — acknowledge competitor strengths, be accurate about your limits; evaluators verify claims.
2. **Depth over surface** — go beyond feature checklists; explain why differences matter; use cases.
3. **Help them decide** — be clear who you're best for and who the competitor is best for.
4. **Modular architecture** — centralize competitor data; single source of truth per competitor; updates propagate.

## Four page formats
1. **[Competitor] Alternative (singular)** — intent: actively switching. URL `/alternatives/[competitor]`. Structure: why alternatives → you as the alternative → detailed comparison → who should/shouldn't switch → migration path → switcher proof → CTA.
2. **[Competitor] Alternatives (plural)** — intent: researching, earlier. URL `/alternatives/[competitor]-alternatives`. Include 4-7 real alternatives (you first) — being genuinely helpful ranks better. Structure: why alternatives → selection criteria → list → comparison table → detailed breakdowns → recommendation by use case → CTA.
3. **You vs [Competitor]** — intent: directly comparing. URL `/vs/[competitor]`. Structure: TL;DR → at-a-glance table → category-by-category (features/pricing/support/ease/integrations) → who you're best for → who they're best for (honest) → switcher testimonials → migration → CTA.
4. **[A] vs [B]** — intent: comparing two others. Structure: overview of both → category comparison → who each is best for → introduce yourself as the third option → 3-way table → CTA.

## Essential sections
TL;DR summary · paragraph comparisons (not just tables — explain when each matters) · feature comparison (strengths + limits + bottom line) · pricing comparison (tier-by-tier, hidden costs, sample-team total) · who-it's-for · migration section (what transfers, reconfig, support, switcher quotes).

## Centralized competitor data
One source of truth per competitor: positioning + audience, all pricing tiers, feature ratings, strengths/weaknesses, best/not-ideal for, common complaints (from reviews), migration notes.

## Research process
Per competitor: product research (sign up, use it), pricing research (incl. hidden costs), review mining (G2/Capterra/TrustRadius), customer feedback (both switch directions), content research (their positioning + comparison pages). Updates: quarterly pricing verify, when a customer flags a change, annual full refresh.

## SEO
Keyword targets per format type · internal linking between comparison pages + from feature pages + a hub page · FAQ schema for "best alternative to [Competitor]?"

## Proactive offers
Competitor named in conversation → offer a page set. Sales friction with a tool → offer a vs-page. SEO gap on competitor terms → propose prioritized page set. Switcher testimonial surfaces → build a migration-focused alternative page. Reviewing pricing → note comparison tables belong on competitor pages, not the pricing page.

Every claim must be verifiable from public sources or customer quotes — pages that only disparage competitors lose credibility.
