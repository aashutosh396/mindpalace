# Product Launch Strategy

A launch is a momentum system, not a day. Two frameworks drive everything.

## ORB channel model
Map every launch action to a channel type — a plan covering only one type is incomplete:
- **Owned** — email list, blog, in-app. You control reach; activate first.
- **Rented** — social platforms, communities. Algorithmic reach; play by their rules.
- **Borrowed** — partner audiences, newsletters, podcasts, Product Hunt. Other people's reach; requires relationship work weeks ahead.

## Phase model
1. **Pre-launch (2-6 weeks out)** — waitlist/early access, borrowed-channel outreach, asset production.
2. **Launch day** — time-boxed checklist, all channels firing, founder available for engagement.
3. **Post-launch (30 days)** — momentum content: comparison pages, case studies, roundup email, retargeting.

## Readiness gating
Score the launch checklist when planning starts and again one week out. Below a passing score in "owned channels ready" or "assets ready" → slip the date, don't hope.

## Questions to ask
What's launching (new product / major feature / minor update)? Current audience size + engagement? Owned channels (list size, blog traffic, community)? Timeline? Past launch learnings? Product Hunt — considering it, prep status?

## Proactive offers
- Feature ship date mentioned → ask about the launch plan immediately.
- Waitlist/early access mentioned → design the full phased funnel (alpha → GA).
- Product Hunt mentioned → trigger full PH strategy incl. pre-launch relationship timeline.
- Post-launch silence → suggest momentum content (comparison pages, roundup email, demo).
- Pricing change planned → treat as a launch; build an announcement campaign.

## Output artifacts
Launch plan (phase-by-phase with owners/dates/channels/metrics) · ORB channel map · launch-day checklist (time-boxed) · Product Hunt brief (listing copy, asset specs, pre-launch timeline, engagement playbook) · 30-day post-launch momentum plan.

Every output is concrete, time-bound, channel-specific — who does what and when, no vague "post on social media." A launch plan is complete only when it covers all three ORB channel types and both launch-day and post-launch actions.
