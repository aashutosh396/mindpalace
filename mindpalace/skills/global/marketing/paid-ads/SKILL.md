# Paid Ads

Performance marketing: create, optimize, scale campaigns that drive efficient acquisition. Always confirm conversion tracking is in place before recommending creative/targeting changes — a campaign without attribution is guesswork.

## Gather context
Goals (objective, target CPA/ROAS, budget, constraints) · product & offer (what's promoted, landing page, why compelling) · audience (ICP, problem, search/interest, customer data for lookalikes) · current state (past results, pixel/conversion data, funnel CVR).

## Platform selection
Google Ads (high-intent search) · Meta (demand gen, visual, B2C) · LinkedIn (B2B, job-title targeting, higher ACV) · X (tech, timely) · TikTok (younger, video).

## Campaign structure
Account → Campaign ([objective]-[audience/product]) → Ad Set ([targeting variation]) → Ads (3 creative variations). Naming: `[Platform]_[Objective]_[Audience]_[Offer]_[Date]`. Budget: testing phase (2-4 wks) 70% proven / 30% testing; scaling phase consolidate into winners, increase 20-30% at a time, wait 3-5 days between increases for learning.

## Ad copy frameworks
PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve) · BAB (Before-After-Bridge) · Social Proof Lead (stat/testimonial → what you do → CTA).

## Targeting
Google = keywords/intent · Meta = interests/behaviors/lookalikes · LinkedIn = job titles/companies. Lookalikes base on best customers by LTV (not all customers). Retargeting segment by funnel stage. Always exclude existing customers + recent converters.

## Creative
Image: clear UI screenshots, before/after, stats as focal point, real faces, <20% text overlay. Video (15-30s): hook (0-3s) → problem (3-8s) → solution (8-20s) → CTA (20-30s); captions always (85% watch silent), vertical for stories/reels, native feel. Testing hierarchy by impact: concept/angle > hook/headline > visual > body > CTA.

## Optimization levers
- **CPA too high**: check landing page (post-click?), tighten targeting, new creative angles, improve quality score, adjust bid.
- **CTR low**: creative not resonating / audience mismatch / fatigue.
- **CPM high**: too narrow / high competition / low relevance.
- **Bid strategy**: start manual/cost-caps → gather 50+ conversions → switch to automated with data-based targets.

## Retargeting (funnel-based)
Top (blog/video viewers) = educational/social proof. Middle (pricing/feature visitors) = case studies/demos. Bottom (cart/trial) = urgency/objection handling. Windows: hot 1-7d, warm 7-30d (3-5x/wk), cold 30-90d (1-2x/wk). Exclude existing customers, recent converters (7-14d), <10s bounces, irrelevant pages.

## Reporting
Weekly: spend vs budget pacing · CPA/ROAS vs targets (margin-adjusted, not platform-reported) · account health trend · top/bottom ads · audience breakdown · frequency (fatigue) · LP conversion. Platform attribution is inflated — use consistent UTMs, compare to GA4, watch blended CAC.

## Common mistakes
Launching without tracking · too many campaigns (fragmented budget) · not enough learning time · wrong optimization metric · audiences too narrow/broad · not excluding customers · one ad per ad set · creative fatigue · big budget jumps that disrupt learning.

## Proactive flags
ROAS dropping → check creative fatigue + frequency first. First campaign → run pre-launch checklist (tracking, LP speed, UTMs). High CTR low conversion → diagnose landing page, not the ad. Aggressive scaling → warn about learning-phase disruption. B2B lead gen → LinkedIn for job-title targeting (higher CPL, better quality).
