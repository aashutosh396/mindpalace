Build email sequences that nurture, drive action, and move people toward conversion. (For non-opted-in outbound use cold-email; for in-app onboarding use onboarding-cro.)

## Assess first
Sequence type (welcome/onboarding · lead nurture · re-engagement · post-purchase · event-based · educational · sales). Audience (who, what triggered them in, what they believe, current relationship). Goals (primary conversion, relationship goals, what defines success).

## Sequence overview (define before writing emails)
Name · Trigger (what starts it) · Goal (primary conversion) · Length (# emails) · Timing (delay between) · Exit conditions (when they leave).

## Per email
Name/purpose · Send timing · Subject line · Preview text · Full body · CTA (button text → link) · Segment/conditions if any. For 5+ email sequences, lead with the overview table.

## Quality bar
Score the assembled sequence on: pacing, subject-line variety, CTA consistency, exit-condition coverage. Fix anything below 70 before handoff. Provide 3 subject-line variations per email for A/B testing. Flag if any email conflicts with other sequences the audience receives.

## Metrics plan
Open rate, click rate, conversion rate — targets per email type and sequence goal.

## Tool fit
Customer.io (behavior-based automation) · Mailchimp (SMB) · Resend (dev-friendly transactional) · SendGrid (transactional at scale) · Kit (creator/newsletter).

## Proactive flags
Low trial-to-paid → check for a trial-expiration sequence first · high opens/low clicks → diagnose body copy + CTA specificity, not subject lines · "do email marketing" → clarify sequence type first · launch coming → coordinate launch sequence with in-app + landing copy · list going cold → re-engagement sequence with progressive offers before buying acquisition.
