# Strategic Alignment Engine

Strategy fails at the cascade, not the boardroom. The further a goal gets from the strategy that created it, the less it reflects original intent (organizational telephone game).

## 6-step framework

### Step 1 — Strategy articulation test
Ask five people from five teams: "What's the company's most important strategic priority right now?" All five same → ✅ clear. 3–4 similar → 🟡 clarify. <3 agree → 🔴 strategy isn't clear enough to cascade; fix this first. **Format test:** statable in one sentence. ❌ "PLG while maintaining enterprise and expanding international and investing in platform." ✅ "Win the mid-market healthcare segment in DACH before Series B."

### Step 2 — Cascade mapping
Map company OKRs → dept → team → individual. For each goal: which company goal does it support? If 100% achieved, how much does it move the parent? Direct or theoretical connection?

### Step 3 — Detection (3 failure patterns)
**Orphan goals** — connect to no company goal. Fix: connect or cut; every goal needs a parent. **Conflicting goals** — both succeeding makes things worse (sales closes bad-fit volume, CS satisfaction tanks). Fix: cross-functional OKR review pre-quarter, shared metrics where teams interact. **Coverage gaps** — a company OKR no team supports. Fix: explicit ownership; unowned OKRs don't happen.

### Step 4 — Silo identification
Signals: a dept hits goals while company misses · teams don't know others' work · "not our problem" · escalations flow up but coordination never sideways · data not shared. Root causes: incentive misalignment, no shared goals, no shared language, geography/time zones.

### Step 5 — Communication gap analysis
Message decays: CEO → managers filter → teams modify → individuals interpret. Sources: ambiguity (too high-level), insufficient frequency (one all-hands/quarter), medium mismatch, trust deficit. Detect: run the Step-1 test at all levels; compare what leadership thinks it said vs what teams heard.

### Step 6 — Realignment protocol
(a) Don't start with what's wrong (creates defensiveness) — start with "here's where we're heading." (b) Re-cascade in a workshop, not a memo. (c) Fix incentives before goals — if dept heads are rewarded for conflicting local metrics, no goal-setting fixes it. (d) Install a quarterly alignment check.

## Alignment score (0–10 each, /50)

Strategy clarity · cascade completeness · conflict detection · coverage (each OKR owned) · communication (behaviors reflect strategy). 45–50 excellent · 35–44 good · 20–34 misalignment costing you · <20 strategic drift = crisis.
