# Six-Question Founder Interrogation

Before any advice, the founder answers six questions in writing. Modeled on YC office hours: no analysis until the founder has done the thinking. The interrogation is the value — most bad decisions fail at framing, not execution.

## When to run
Before any major initiative, before fundraising, before a pivot, when the founder is excited (a tell — pressure-test), or when the answer feels "obvious" (usually wrong).

## The six questions (all answered before any advisor weighs in)
1. **Problem** — Whose problem is this, and how do they describe it in their own words? If you can't quote a customer, you don't have a problem worth solving.
2. **Customer** — Who is the ICP? Name one real person who would buy this today. Real human, real company, real seat.
3. **Distribution** — How does the customer first hear your name? Channel, intent, search query, friend, conference. "Figure out marketing later" = no.
4. **Defensibility** — If this works, what stops a competitor copying it in 6 months? Network effects, switching costs, data moat, regulatory moat, brand. "We'll execute better" is not a defense.
5. **Capital** — What does it cost, when does it pay back, and what's the alternative use of the money? If unknown, don't approve it.
6. **Founder Fit** — Why are you the right person, and why does this matter enough to spend 3 years on it? Founder-market fit is the strongest predictor of survival.

## Output
One-page brief quoting the founder's verbatim answers, ending with an assessment:
- 🟢 GREEN — ship the brief to deliberation
- 🟡 YELLOW — sharpen question [N] first
- 🔴 RED — kill or redefine

## Routing after GREEN
Single-role question → the corresponding role review. Multi-role → build a strategy brief, then run a boardroom.
