# Change Management Playbook

Most changes fail at implementation, not design. ADKAR tells you why and how to fix it. Each stage must be complete before the next sticks.

## ADKAR (startup-speed)
- **Awareness** — people understand WHY (the business reason), not just the announcement. Mistake: WHAT before WHY. They need: what problem, why now, who decided + how. Shortcut: a 5-min CEO "why" video beats a formal doc.
- **Desire** — people want it (or don't actively resist). Awareness ≠ desire. Creates desire: honest "what's in it for me" per group, involving people in the "how", addressing fears directly. Destroys it: pretending it's better for everyone, ignoring legitimate losses, no consultation. Shortcut: a "concerns and questions" session within 48h.
- **Knowledge** — people know HOW to operate in the new world. Mistake: announce and assume. Need: step-by-step docs, training before go-live, "what do I do when [scenario]?", who to ask. Methods: live training (skills/complex tools), docs (always), video (tools, 24/7), shadowing (behavior, weeks 2-4), office hours (edge cases, first 4-6 weeks).
- **Ability** — time, tools, support to actually do it. Training = knowledge; ability = practice. Need: time to practice before evaluation, safe environment for mistakes, reduced load during transition, access to help. Gap signs: reverting under pressure, workarounds emerging, high training scores but unchanged behavior.
- **Reinforcement** — the change sticks. Mistake: declare victory at go-live. Need: visible measurement, recognition of early adopters, leader modeling, removing the old option, consequences for non-adoption. **Compliance** (does it when watched) vs **adoption** (believes it's better) — only reinforcement creates adoption.

## By Change Type (timeline · hardest phase · critical reinforcement)
- **Process change** — 4-8wk · Ability · deprecate the old tool. Sequence: wk-2 announce why+date, wk-1 training, wk0 launch+point-person, wk2 adoption check, wk4 feedback+wins, wk8 old system off.
- **Org change (reorg/new leader)** — 3-6mo · Desire · consistent new-leadership behavior. Sequence: day0 announce why (synchronous), day1 1:1s, wk1 honest FAQ, wk2-4 operate, mo2 retro, mo3-6 health check-ins.
- **Strategy pivot** — 3-12mo · Awareness (people don't believe it's real) · visible resource reallocation. Internal first always; reallocate budget within 2 weeks or nobody believes it.
- **Culture change** — 12-24mo · Reinforcement · visible decisions reflecting new values. Build with input, anchor each value to observable behavior, leaders model first, integrate into reviews.

## Resistance Patterns (resistance is information, not defiance)
"This won't work" → awareness/credibility gap → show evidence. "Why now?" → awareness → explain urgency. "I wasn't consulted" → desire → involve in the how now. "No time" → ability → reduce load. "We tried this before" → trust → be specific about what's different. Silent non-compliance → 1:1 to diagnose. Worst response: dismissing resistance as a personality flaw.

## Change Fatigue
Signals: eye-rolls, low session attendance, fast paper-compliance + slow adoption. Prevention: finish what you start; one significant change at a time (2-3mo stability between); announce what's NOT changing; show prior results before the next change. Already in it: pause non-critical changes, run a change inventory, prioritize ruthlessly.

## Red Flags
Friday-afternoon announcements; "questions not welcome"; no safe FAQ; old system still running 6wk after go-live; leaders exempted from the change; no adoption measurement.
