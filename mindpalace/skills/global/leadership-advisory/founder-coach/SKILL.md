# Founder Development Coach

The company grows only as fast as the founder. The founder is always the structural constraint. Ceilings hit at ~15 people (can't be in every meeting and still think), ~50 (style creates culture problems), ~150 (need a real exec team or you become the scaling blocker).

## 1. Archetype

| Archetype | Strength | Blind spot | Needs |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-------|
| Builder | Product/eng depth | GTM, storytelling, people | A seller/GTM partner |
| Seller | Revenue, relationships, vision | Ops, process | An operator/COO |
| Operator | Execution, reliability | Vision, risk | A visionary co-founder |
| Visionary | Strategy, narrative | Execution, details | An integrator/COO |

Probes: what you do with a free hour, what you procrastinate on, what the team says you don't do.

## 2. Delegation

**Skill × Will matrix:** high/high → delegate fully; high will/low skill → coach; high skill/low will → fix environment or accept mismatch; low/low → don't delegate, address performance.
**Delegation ladder:** (1) do exactly what I say → (2) research & report → (3) propose, I decide → (4) decide & tell me → (5) handle it, update only if out of bounds. Most founders never get past level 3 — that's the bottleneck.
**Delegate first:** recurring ops, info gathering, scheduling, reports. **Last:** strategic pivots, exec hires, large commitments, M&A.

## 3. Energy management

Map the week by energy (🟢 energizing / 🟡 neutral / 🔴 draining). Maximize green, delegate red, accept yellow. Protect 2–4h deep-work blocks 3–5 days/week. Batch shallow work twice daily. Schedule hardest work in your peak window.

## 4. CEO calendar audit

Pull last 4 weeks; categorize: Strategy 20–25% · People 20–25% · External 20% · Execution 15% · Admin <15% · Recovery 10–15%. Red flags: admin >20% (you're a coordinator), execution >30% (still an IC), people <10% (team starving), no recovery, strategy <10%. CEO focus by stage: Seed=product/customers; A=hire exec team; B=culture/strategy/external; C+=vision/board/narrative.

## 5. Leadership evolution

IC→Manager (0–10): teach, build trust. Manager→Leader (10–50, the hardest): stop doing it yourself, trust people you barely know. Leader→Executive (50–200): set culture/direction, communicate obsessively. →Institutional CEO (200+): build systems that work without you.

## 6. Blind spots

Common: "I said it once" · decision speed teams can't track · context hoarding · optimism bias · founder exceptionalism · feedback avoidance. Find them via anonymous 360s, exit-interview patterns, failure post-mortems, energy audit.

## 7. Imposter syndrome

It evolves with stretch — if you never feel it, you're not growing. Keep an evidence file; separate feeling from fact; do the thing anyway; name it to a trusted person.

## 8. Mental health / burnout

Signals: early (irritability, sleep, lost enthusiasm) → mid (physical symptoms, cynicism, priority paralysis) → late (can't function). Prevent: protected recovery time, therapy/coaching, founder peer group, clear off-ramps.

## 9. Founder-mode trap

Helps in crisis, PMF search, irreversible decisions, early stage. Hurts when it undermines hired managers, is driven by distrust, prevents team judgment, or you just miss doing. **Test:** going deep because the situation requires it (leadership) or because you can't let go (the trap)?

## 10. Succession

Levels: 0 (nothing) → 1 (knowledge documented) → 2 (someone covers each function 2 weeks) → 3 (team runs the company a quarter) → 4 (successor identified). Target level 2; level 3 is a strategic asset.
