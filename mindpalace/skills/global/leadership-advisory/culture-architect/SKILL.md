# Culture Architect

Culture is what you DO, not what you SAY. Build it as an operational system: observable behaviors, measurable health, scalable rituals.

**Core principle:** Culture = (what you reward) + (what you tolerate) + (what you celebrate). If values say "transparency" but you punish bearers of bad news, your real value is "optics." Culture is descriptive, not aspirational — the work is closing the gap between stated and actual.

## Frameworks

**1. Mission / Vision / Values workshop** (conversational, not corporate offsite). Mission = why we exist beyond money, present-tense, specific ("We reduce preventable falls in elderly care", not "to be the leading…"). Vision = winning in 5-10yr, specific enough to be wrong. Values = behaviors you actually model — start from what you observe ("what did our last great hire do nobody asked them to?"), keep to 3-5.

**2. Values → behaviors translation (the work).** Every value needs a behavioral anchor or it's decoration. "Transparency" → "we share bad news within 24h, including to our manager." "Speed" → "decisions under €5K happen at team level, same day, no approval." Workshop test: "how would a new hire know we live this on day 30?" If you can't answer concretely, it's an aspiration, not a value.

**3. Culture code.** Public doc describing how you operate — should scare off the wrong people and attract the right ones. Structure: who we are / who thrives here / **who doesn't thrive here** (honest — the useful part) / how we decide / how we communicate / how we grow people / what we expect of leaders. Anti-patterns: "we're a family" (families don't fire for performance); only positive traits; aspirational not descriptive.

**4. Culture health assessment** (quarterly, anonymous, 8-12 questions). Measure: psychological safety, clarity, fairness, growth, trust in leadership. Score: 80-100% healthy / 65-79% warning / 50-64% damaged (urgent) / <50% crisis (all-hands intervention).

**5. Rituals by stage.** Seed (<15): weekly all-hands, monthly retro, default-to-transparency. Early growth (15-50): quarterly survey, recognition ritual tied to values, onboarding buddy, leadership office hours. Scaling (50-200): peer culture committee, values-based reviews, manager training, separate dept + company all-hands. Large (200+): annual culture plan with owner + KPIs, culture NPS, subculture management.

## Anti-Patterns
**Value-washing** (listing values you don't practice — fix: audit "what did the last promoted person demonstrate?"). **Culture debt** (tolerated compromises compound — fix: act on violations faster than feels necessary; one tolerated bad behavior destroys ten good ones). **Founder culture trap** (frozen at founders' personality — fix: explicitly evolve values as you scale). **Culture by osmosis** (assuming it transmits naturally past 10 people — fix: document, teach, measure, reward it).

## Key Questions
"Name the last person we fired for culture reasons — what did they do?" "What behavior got your last promotion? Is it in your values?" "What would a new hire observe day 1 that tells them what's really valued?" "What do we tolerate that we shouldn't?"
