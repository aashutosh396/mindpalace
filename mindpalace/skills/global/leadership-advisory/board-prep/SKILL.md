# Board-Prep — Prepare for the Adversarial Board, Not the Friendly One

Board members have seen 50+ companies; they know when you're not being straight. The best meetings aren't where everything looks good — they're where the CEO shows they see reality clearly, have a plan, and execute under pressure.

## The Framework

**Phase 1 — Numbers cold.** Every number lives in your head, not just the slide: MRR/ARR + MoM growth, burn + runway, headcount by dept, CAC/LTV by channel, NRR, pipeline (value/conversion/cycle), churn (rate/reasons/top accounts), gross + net margin, open key roles + time-to-fill. Stress test: can you answer "what's your burn?" without pausing? A pause means you don't know it.

**Phase 2 — Anticipate hard questions.** For each agenda item, generate the adversarial version. Examples — Revenue: "You missed by 20%. What specifically failed — pipeline, conversion, or capacity?" Runway: "What would you cut first to extend 6 months today?" Product: "You shipped X. What did customers actually do with it? What did you kill?" Team: "Who's at risk of leaving? 3 VP hires didn't work — what pattern do you see?" Competition: "They raised $50M. If they copy your best feature in 90 days, what's your moat?"

**Phase 3 — Build the narrative** (leadership demonstration, not status update): Where we are (honest, the real number) → What we learned → What we got wrong (name it, don't make them ask) → What we're doing (specific/dated/owned) → What we need from this room (concrete ask). **Rule on bad news:** never let the board be surprised — a 5-sentence email 3 days before.

**Phase 4 — Adversarial rehearsal.** Mock meeting; someone plays the hardest director; answer without the deck. The questions that made you pause or defensive = the ones to prepare.

**Phase 5 — Director-by-director.** Per director: primary concern (tied to their thesis), the metric they watch, what would lose their confidence, what they said last time. Types: operator (what's breaking + who owns the fix), financial (path to profit/raise), strategic (moat/competition), independent (governance/judgment).

## Pre-Meeting Checklist
48h before: numbers verified against source systems; deck internally consistent; pre-read sent; 1:1s with concerned directors; 3 hardest questions rehearsed out loud. Day of: agenda with time allocations; the ask per item (decision/input/FYI); follow-up template ready.

## During / After
During — the board watches: do you own bad news or deflect? Defend a narrative or share reality? Know your numbers or look them up? Best move: name the hard thing before they do. After (24h): action items with owners/dates, promised data, questions you weren't ready for, follow-up with any unsatisfied director.
