# International Expansion

Decision sequence: Market selection → Entry mode → Regulatory assessment → Localization plan → GTM strategy → Team structure → Launch.

## Market selection scoring matrix

| Factor | Weight | Assess |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Market size (addressable) | 25% | TAM in target segment, willingness to pay |
| Competitive intensity | 20% | Incumbent strength, market gaps |
| Regulatory complexity | 20% | Barriers, compliance cost, timeline |
| Cultural distance | 15% | Language, business practices, buying behavior |
| Existing traction | 10% | Inbound, existing customers, partnerships |
| Operational complexity | 10% | Time zones, infrastructure, payments |

## Entry modes (graduate as revenue justifies)

| Mode | Investment | Control | Best for |
|------|-----------|---------|----------|
| Export (sell remotely) | Low | Low | Testing demand |
| Partnership (reseller/distributor) | Medium | Medium | Strong local requirements |
| Local team (hire in-market) | High | High | Strategic markets, proven demand |
| Entity (full subsidiary) | Very high | Full | Major / regulatory-required markets |
| Acquisition | Highest | Full | Fast entry with existing base |

**Default path:** Export → Partnership → Local team → Entity.

## Localization checklist

**Product:** language (UI/docs/support), local currency + pricing (not just conversion), local payment methods, date/time/number formats, data residency/privacy, cultural adaptation.
**GTM:** message adaptation, local channel strategy, local case studies/social proof, local partnerships, event presence, local SEO/content.
**Ops:** legal entity, tax compliance, employment law, support hours/language, banking/payments.

## Key questions

- Is there pull from the market, or are we pushing?
- Cost of entry vs 3-year revenue opportunity?
- Can we serve from HQ, or need boots on the ground?
- Regulatory timeline — can we launch before paperwork is done?
- Who's winning and what would it take to displace them?

## Common mistakes

Entering too many markets at once (cap at 1–2/year) · copy-paste GTM from home market (research local buying behavior) · underestimating regulatory cost (assess BEFORE committing) · hiring too early (prove demand first) · wrong pricing by just converting (research local willingness to pay).
