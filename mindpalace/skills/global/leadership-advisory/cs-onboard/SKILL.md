# Founder Onboarding Interview

One ~45-min conversation that builds the company-context file every advisor reads. Refresh quarterly (~15 min).

## Conversation principles

Be a conversation, not an interrogation. One question at a time. Follow threads. Reflect back: "So the real issue is X — right?" Watch what they skip — that's where the real story lives. Never read a list of questions. Open with: *"Tell me about the company in your own words — what are you building and why does it matter?"*

## 7 interview dimensions

1. **Company identity** — what they do, who for, the real "why," one-sentence pitch, non-negotiable values. Probe: *"What's a value you'd fire someone over violating?"* Red flag: values that sound like marketing copy.
2. **Stage & scale** — headcount (FT vs contractor), revenue range, runway, stage (pre-PMF / scaling / optimizing), what broke in last 90 days. Probe: *"Still finding PMF, scaling what works, or optimizing?"*
3. **Founder profile** — superpower, blind spots, archetype, what keeps them up at night. Probe: *"What would your co-founder say you should stop doing?"* Red flag: no blind spots, or a weakness framed as a strength.
4. **Team & culture** — team in 3 words, last real conflict + resolution, real vs aspirational values, strongest/weakest leader. Probe: *"Which value is real? Which is a poster?"* Red flag: "we have no conflict."
5. **Market & competition** — who's winning and why (honest), real unfair advantage, the one move that could hurt them. Probe: *"Real unfair advantage — not the investor version?"* Red flag: "we have no real competition."
6. **Current challenges** — priority stack-rank (product/growth/people/money/ops), the avoided decision, the "one extra day" answer. Probe: *"What decision have you put off for weeks?"*
7. **Goals & ambition** — 12-month (specific) + 36-month (directional) targets, exit vs build-forever, personal success definition.

## Output: company-context.md

Fill every section; write `[not captured]` for unknowns — never blank. Add timestamp, mark `fresh`. Single source of truth — don't duplicate.

## Quarterly refresh

Trigger every 90 days or after a major change. Open: *"It's been [X] since your context. What's changed?"* Walk each dimension with one "what changed?" question. Update file, refresh timestamp, reset to `fresh`.
