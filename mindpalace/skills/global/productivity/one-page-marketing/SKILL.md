# The 1-Page Marketing Plan

Allan Dib's 9-square grid: a complete marketing system on one page. Three phases × three steps turn a stranger into a raving fan. Marketing is a process, not an event.

## Core Principle

Replace disconnected tactics with a sequential process across **BEFORE** (find the prospect), **DURING** (capture, nurture, convert the lead), and **AFTER** (deliver, maximize value, generate referrals from the customer). When all nine squares work together you have a marketing machine.

## Scoring

Rate the plan 0-10 by how completely and specifically all nine squares are filled. 9-10 = each square specific, measurable, execution-ready. State score and gaps (the AFTER phase is usually missing).

## The 9 Squares

### BEFORE — Prospect
1. **Target Market** — pick a niche you can dominate using the **PVP Index** (Personal fulfillment, Value to marketplace, Profitability). The riches are in the niches; if you speak to everyone you speak to no one. Build a detailed customer avatar (demographics, psychographics, pains, desires, watering holes).
2. **Message / USP** — answer "Why buy from you over your nearest competitor?" A USP is a defensible, provable position (specialization, unique mechanism, bold guarantee, proprietary process), not a slogan. Swap test: if a competitor's name fits your message, it's too generic. Elevator pitch: "You know how [target] struggles with [problem]? We [solution] so they can [outcome]."
3. **Media** — apply direct-response principles: trackable, measurable, action-provoking — never vague "brand awareness." Pick channels where the target actually is; master one before adding another; track CAC per channel vs LTV.

### DURING — Lead
4. **Capture Leads** — goal isn't the immediate sale; it's a database. Only ~3% are ready to buy now; capture the other 97% with a lead magnet that solves a specific immediate problem. Use a CRM from day one. Speed-to-lead: respond within 5 minutes.
5. **Nurture** — most leads aren't ready; build the relationship with consistent value, education, and trust until they are (email sequences, content, retargeting).
6. **Convert** — remove friction, overcome objections, build trust with social proof and guarantees, make it easy to say yes (risk reversal, clear next step).

### AFTER — Customer
7. **World-Class Experience** — the sale is the starting line. Systematize for consistency; design "moments of truth" that surprise and delight; turn customers into a tribe of advocates.
8. **Increase Lifetime Value** — the first sale is the most expensive; upsells, cross-sells, an ascension model, and retention are the highest-leverage activity. Price tiers and recurring revenue.
9. **Orchestrate Referrals** — don't leave referrals to chance; design a systematic, rewarding, easy program plus strategic partnerships for exponential reach.

## Ethics
Choose niches you can genuinely serve (never target vulnerable populations for exploitation). USP must be truthful and deliverable. All advertising truthful and compliant — no deceptive claims or bait-and-switch.
