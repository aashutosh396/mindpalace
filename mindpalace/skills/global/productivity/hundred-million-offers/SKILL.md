# Grand Slam Offer Creation Framework

Alex Hormozi's method for building offers so good people feel stupid saying no. The offer matters more than how or who you sell to.

## Core Principle

**The offer is the #1 lever:** a Grand Slam Offer sells despite mediocre marketing; the best marketing can't save a bad offer. Maximize Dream Outcome and Perceived Likelihood while minimizing Time Delay and Effort — becoming a category of one with no comparable alternative. Fix the offer before optimizing funnels or ads.

## Scoring

Rate any offer 0-10: a 10 is genuinely irresistible — high perceived value, reversed risk, ethical scarcity, compelling bonuses, attention-grabbing name. State score and fixes.

## Framework

### 1. The Value Equation
**Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice).** Maximize numerator, minimize denominator. Perceived Likelihood often matters more than actual results — social proof, guarantees, track record raise it. Time Delay is a silent killer; faster results command premium prices. A guarantee raises likelihood and lowers risk at once. Copy: "Get [outcome] in [short time] without [effort]."

### 2. The Grand Slam Offer
A complete package — core offer + bonuses + guarantee + scarcity + urgency + a compelling name. Bundling makes price comparison impossible (escape commoditization). List every problem between customer and Dream Outcome; create a solution + delivery vehicle for each. **Trim & Stack:** cut low-value/high-cost, stack high-value/low-cost. Sum of component values should be ≥10x the price.

### 3. Find Your Starving Crowd
The best offer fails in the wrong market. Four criteria: massive pain, purchasing power, easy to target, growing. Pain matters most — people pay to stop pain faster than to gain pleasure. Niching down raises perceived value (specificity signals expertise).

### 4. Value-Based Pricing
Charge on value delivered, not cost; aim for 10:1 value-to-price. Low prices attract churny, price-sensitive customers; premium prices attract committed ones who get better results and fund great delivery. Raising prices often increases conversions (signals quality). Anchor against the cost of NOT solving the problem. Payment plans remove price as an objection.

### 5. Bonuses (Value Stacking)
Each bonus kills a specific objection or obstacle. Present the most valuable bonus first as the anchor. Partner bonuses add value at zero cost. Name each bonus; keep them high-value / low-cost-to-deliver (templates, recordings, access). When total bonus value exceeds price, the core product feels "free."

### 6. Guarantees (Reverse Risk)
Transfer risk from buyer to seller — the biggest fear isn't losing money, it's making a bad decision. Types: unconditional ("money back, no questions"), conditional ("do X, get result or refund"), anti-guarantee, implied/performance ("only pay when you get leads"). A strong guarantee makes "yes" psychologically safe.

### 7. Scarcity & Urgency
**Scarcity** limits quantity (how many); **urgency** limits time (how long). Both give people who already want it a reason to act now. Must be genuine — fake countdowns destroy trust.

### 8. Naming (MAGIC formula)
- **M** = Magnetic reason why (hook, event, season, trend)
- **A** = Avatar (who it's for — specific)
- **G** = Goal (Dream Outcome, concrete)
- **I** = Indicate a time frame (how fast)
- **C** = Container word (challenge, blueprint, accelerator, bootcamp, system, formula, masterclass)
Pattern: "The [Time Frame] [Avatar] [Goal] [Container]" — e.g. "The 6-Figure Freelancer Blueprint: From $5K to $15K Months in 120 Days."

## Ethics
Never promise outcomes you can't reasonably deliver — substantiate speed/effort/results claims with real data. Assign honest, defensible dollar values to components (no fake value-price gaps). Target genuine need and fit, never vulnerability. Scarcity and urgency must be real.
