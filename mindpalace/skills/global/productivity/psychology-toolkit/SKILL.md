# Psychology Toolkit

Cognitive and behavioral frameworks for understanding why people think, feel, and act as they do — and how to influence and change that. Confirm the person/group, the behavior or decision at issue, and the goal in one sentence, then route.

## Which tool fits

| You need to... | Tool |
|---|---|
| Identify biases distorting thinking in this case | Cognitive Biases |
| Diagnose what's really driving behavior | Motivation |
| Decide whether to trust or override your gut | Heuristics |
| Change a mind or drive action | Persuasion |
| Shift entrenched behavior, build/break a habit | Behavior Change |

When unclear → Motivation; most behavior questions are ultimately about what need is being served.

## Cognitive Biases

A targeted diagnostic, not a laundry list. Identify the decision/belief/behavior, scan for which bias categories are most live given the context, diagnose how the distortion operates, recommend the counter-move. Key categories: confirmation, availability, anchoring, sunk cost, in-group, optimism, planning fallacy.
**Output:** Active biases table with distortion mechanism and counter-move for each.

## Motivation

Name the surface motivation, then probe the fundamental need it serves. Apply Self-Determination Theory (autonomy, competence, relatedness), Maslow as a diagnostic (unmet lower-level need?), and identity ("I'm the kind of person who…").
**Output:** Surface motivation, root motivation, underlying need, and the lever that addresses the real driver.

## Heuristics

Identify the fast-thinking pattern (representativeness, availability, affect, recognition). Is it in its domain of reliability? Is the situation engineered to exploit it? Kahneman System 1/System 2: System 1 is adaptive within experience, fails outside it.
**Output:** Heuristic identified, domain assessment, distortion risk, and trust/override recommendation.

## Persuasion

Identify what you're changing (belief, attitude, behavior, decision). Select the approach: Cialdini's six (reciprocity, commitment/consistency, social proof, authority, liking, scarcity), elaboration likelihood (central route for motivated/capable audiences; peripheral for low-engagement), or inoculation. Note ethical constraints.
**Output:** Influence analysis, recommended approach, ethical assessment, and the constructed message.

## Behavior Change

Classify what maintains the behavior: habit loop (cue → routine → reward), motivation deficit, capability gap, friction, or identity conflict. Select intervention: habit redesign, implementation intentions, friction reduction, motivation amplification, or identity reframing.
**Output:** Maintenance diagnosis, intervention type, and specific implementation design.
