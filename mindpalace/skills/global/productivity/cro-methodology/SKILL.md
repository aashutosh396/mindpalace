# CRO Methodology

Conversion Rate Experts' evidence-based CRO. Big wins come from understanding WHY visitors don't convert, not from copying competitors or generic tips.

## Core Principle

**Don't guess — discover.** Every non-converting visitor has a reason. Discover those reasons through research, then eliminate them with evidence and proof. This beats "best practices," intuition, and HiPPO (highest-paid-person's-opinion) decisions.

## Scoring

Rate any page/funnel 0-10 against the principles below. State score and fixes.

## Frameworks

### 1. The CRO Process
Define success metrics aligned to KPIs → map the whole funnel for "blocked arteries" (high-traffic underperformers) and "missing links" → research visitors three ways (who they are, what blocks them UX-wise, what objections stop them) → gather market intelligence → prioritize with ICE → design bold experiments → run with statistical rigor → scale wins everywhere.

### 2. Customer Research & Objections
Teams' guesses about why visitors leave are almost always wrong. Mine exit surveys, chat logs, support tickets, sales calls, reviews. Quantitative (analytics, heatmaps) shows WHERE; qualitative (surveys, interviews) shows WHY. The customer's own language out-persuades any copywriter. Non-converter surveys: ask ONE question. Post-purchase: "What almost stopped you from buying?"
- **Big 5 universal objections:** Trust, Price, Fit, Timing, Effort.

### 3. Persuasion Assets
Visitors decide on evidence, not claims. Audit five categories: Credentials/Authority, Social Proof, Risk Reversal, Data/Specificity, Process/Methodology. Acquire missing ones. **Proof sandwich:** Claim → Proof → Reinforcement. Proof hierarchy (strongest first): specific results with context > named testimonials w/ photos > case studies > statistics > logos > generic testimonials. Place proof at points of friction; specific numbers beat round ("47,832" beats "~50,000").

### 4. The O/CO Framework
Map every objection to a specific evidence-backed counter-objection, placed where the objection arises (credit-card concern near the payment form, not in an FAQ). Implicit objections need "CO Only" — counter without stating the objection.
| Objection | Counter |
|---|---|
| Trust | named testimonials, media logos, awards, guarantee |
| Price | ROI calc, cost vs alternatives, payment plans |
| Fit | similar-customer case studies, segmented pages, free trial |
| Timing | cost-of-delay math, genuine limited offers |
| Effort | "done for you", "set up in 5 minutes", step-by-step |

### 5. Hypothesis Design
Format: **"If we [change X], then [metric Y] will improve because [reason from research]."** Define primary, secondary, and guardrail metrics first. Prioritize with **ICE** (Impact, Confidence, Ease, 1-10 each). Worth testing: redesigns, new value props, different offers. Not worth it: button color, font size. Ask "could this 10x results?"

### 6. A/B Testing Rigor
Calculate required sample size BEFORE starting (baseline, MDE, 80% power, 95% significance). Run ≥1 full business cycle covering weekdays AND weekends. Never peek and stop early (inflates false positives). Mind practical significance — a 0.1% lift may not be worth complexity. Multivariate only at 100k+ monthly visitors. Promote winners to the new control.

## Common Mistakes
Copying competitors blindly · testing button colors before understanding objections · assuming you know why visitors leave · applying unvalidated "best practices" · HiPPO decisions · optimizing pages without funnel context · meek tweaks instead of bold changes · giving up after one failed test.

## Quick-Start Checklist
1. What is the ONE action visitors should take? 2. Who are they / what stage? 3. Top 3-5 objections (researched, not guessed)? 4. What proof counters each? 5. Value prop clear in 5 seconds? 6. UX blockers (speed, mobile, forms)? 7. Which persuasion assets are missing/hidden?

## Ethics
Report all results honestly — never cherry-pick or rerun until you get the answer you want. All proof genuine and verifiable; no fabricated testimonials or fake trust badges. Address real objections, never deceive past valid concerns.
