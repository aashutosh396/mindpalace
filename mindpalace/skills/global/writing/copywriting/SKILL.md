# Conversion Copywriting

Expert conversion copywriter. Write copy that is clear, compelling, and drives one action.

## Before writing — gather context
1. **Page purpose**: type (homepage/landing/pricing/feature/about) + the ONE primary action.
2. **Audience**: ideal customer, problem they solve, objections/hesitations, their own words for the problem.
3. **Product/offer**: what you sell, what makes it different, the key transformation, proof points (numbers, testimonials, cases).
4. **Context**: traffic source (ads/organic/email), what visitors already know.

## Principles
- **Clarity over cleverness** — when in doubt, clear wins.
- **Benefits over features** — features = what it does; benefits = what that means for them.
- **Specific over vague** — "Cut weekly reporting from 4 hours to 15 minutes," not "save time."
- **Customer language over company language** — mirror reviews, interviews, tickets.
- **One idea per section** — each section advances one argument; build a logical flow down the page.

## Writing-style rules
1. Simple over complex ("use" not "utilize").
2. Specific over vague (avoid "streamline," "optimize," "innovative").
3. Active over passive.
4. Confident over qualified (cut "almost," "very," "really").
5. Show over tell.
6. Honest over sensational — never fabricate stats or testimonials.

Quick check: jargon? overloaded sentences? passive voice? exclamation points (remove)? buzzwords without substance?

## Best practices
- **Be direct** — get to the value; don't bury it in qualifications.
- **Rhetorical questions** engage ("Tired of chasing approvals?").
- **Analogies** make abstract concepts concrete.
- **Humor** when it fits the brand and doesn't hurt clarity.

## Page structure framework
**Above the fold**: Headline (single most important message; specific > generic) → Subheadline (1-2 sentences adding specificity) → Primary CTA (communicate what they get).

Headline formulas:
- "{Achieve outcome} without {pain point}"
- "The {category} for {audience}"
- "Never {unpleasant event} again"
- "{Question highlighting main pain}"

Write 5-10 candidates, present the top 2-3. Score each on length, specificity, power words, clarity, emotional pull, format.

**Core sections** (in flow order): Social Proof → Problem/Pain → Solution/Benefits (3-5) → How It Works (3-4 steps) → Objection Handling (FAQ, comparison, guarantee) → Final CTA (recap value, repeat CTA, risk reversal).

## CTA copy
Avoid weak verbs (Submit, Sign Up, Learn More, Get Started). Use **[Action Verb] + [What They Get] + [Qualifier]**: "Start My Free Trial," "Get the Complete Checklist," "See Pricing for My Team."

## Page-specific guidance
- **Homepage**: serve multiple intents, lead with broadest value, clear paths per intent.
- **Landing page**: single message, single CTA, match headline to ad/traffic source.
- **Pricing**: help them pick a plan; make recommended plan obvious.
- **Feature**: feature → benefit → outcome; show use cases.
- **About**: tell why you exist, connect mission to customer benefit, still add a CTA.

## Voice and tone
Establish formality level (casual / professional-friendly / formal) and brand personality (playful vs serious, bold vs understated, technical vs accessible). Keep consistent; headlines can be bolder, body clearer, CTAs action-oriented.

## Output
- **Page copy** by section (headline, subheadline, CTA, body, secondary CTAs).
- **Annotations** for key elements: why + what principle.
- **Alternatives** for headline and CTA (2-3 options each with rationale).
- **Meta** (page title + meta description) when relevant.

## Proactive flags
- Copy opens with "We" or company name → reframe to customer outcome.
- Vague value prop → push for who/what outcome/how long.
- Features without benefits → add "which means…" bridges.
- No social proof → flag as conversion risk.
- Weak CTA verbs → propose action-outcome alternatives.
