# Communication Toolkit

Applies communication thinking to any message, proposal, or delivery decision. Confirm the message, audience, and goal in one sentence, then route.

## Which tool fits

| You need to... | Tool |
|---|---|
| Understand what your audience believes and cares about | Audience Modeling |
| Find where a message will be misread before sending | Clarity Audit |
| Choose the right channel/format | Medium Selection |
| Anticipate and pre-address objections | Objection Mapping |

Routing: don't know the audience / why they're not getting it → Audience Modeling. Have a draft, want to find where it breaks → Clarity Audit. Unsure email vs meet vs async vs document → Medium Selection. About to present something contentious → Objection Mapping. When unclear → Audience Modeling first; communication fails at the receiver, not the sender.

## Audience Modeling

Model the audience before communicating: what do they already believe about this? Their real goals (often different from their stated role)? What do they fear — about the message, the change, the consequences? What would have to be true for them to change their mind or act?
**Output:** Audience belief map, real goals, fears, and the threshold conditions for a positive response.

## Clarity Audit

Read the message as if you have no prior context. Where does it assume knowledge the reader lacks? Where could a word be interpreted differently? Where does structure obscure the point? Where is the ask buried? Classify each problem: ambiguity (multiple valid readings), assumption (missing context), or structure (architecture hides the message).
**Output:** Annotated list of clarity problems by type, with a specific rewrite for each.

## Medium Selection

Assess on four dimensions: complexity (needs dialogue or self-contained?), tone (emotional weight needing presence?), record (must be findable later?), urgency (how time-sensitive?). Map against available channels. The same content in the wrong medium loses most of its effect.
**Output:** Recommended channel with reasoning, format within it, and what to avoid.

## Objection Mapping

List every stakeholder who will encounter the proposal. For each: likely first objection, the deeper concern beneath it, and whether it's addressable by changing the proposal, by framing, or genuinely unresolvable. Anticipated objections feel addressed; surprise objections derail.
**Output:** Objection map — stakeholder, surface objection, underlying concern, how to address — flagging unresolvable objections to prepare for.
