# Hacker News Strategy

HN is the most influential developer community and the easiest to get wrong. Users smell marketing instantly. First check: is this genuinely interesting to hackers, founders, and deep-tech enthusiasts?

## Culture

HN values technical depth over polish, genuine novelty over incremental updates, transparency over corporate speak, building in public, contrarian insights. HN hates self-promotion disguised as content, clickbait, PR speak, engagement bait ("What do you think?"), anything ad-like.

## Post types

**Show HN** — you built something people can try (live demo/download). Must be new, creator available 2-3h in comments. Title: `Show HN: [Name] – [one-line description]`. Good: "Show HN: I built a SQLite extension for vector search". Bad: "Show HN: My startup raised $5M" (nothing to try).

**Launch HN** — major company launch/funding (usually YC-coordinated). Include technical details, share the journey, founders answer personally, no PR speak.

**Regular submissions** — interesting content (yours or others'). Works: deep technical posts, war stories with lessons, problem deep-dives, original data, contrarian takes. Fails: listicles, announcements, product updates, repackaged content.

## Titles

Be specific and factual; state what it is, not why it's great; no superlatives or clickbait. Transform "We Made Deployments 10x Faster" → "How we reduced deployment time from 20min to 2min". Note: mods (dang) edit sensational titles, merge duplicates, and sometimes give worthy posts a second chance.

## Timing & algorithm

Best (PT): 6-9am (catches US East + Europe), 10am-12pm peak. First 1-2 hours of upvotes matter most; comments boost ranking. Too-fast upvotes can trip the flamewar detector; new accounts get scrutiny; controversial posts get penalized.

Kills a post: voting rings (detected, penalized), no early engagement, user flags, duplicate content, repeated self-promotion pattern.

## Comments

If it's your post: post a top-level comment immediately with backstory + technical context, respond to every comment (especially critical ones) in the first 2 hours, keep checking back. Template: "Hey HN, [name] here, [role] at [project]. Backstory: ... Technical context: ... Happy to answer questions about ...".

Responding to criticism: acknowledge valid concerns and explain reasoning; clarify misunderstandings without defensiveness; don't feed trolls; be gracious about competitors. Never get defensive, sarcastic, or use corporate speak.

## What gets flagged/banned

Asking for upvotes anywhere (bannable), voting from same IP/network, multiple accounts upvoting, negatively submitting competitors, astroturfing. Flagged content: obvious marketing, political hot takes, crypto/Web3 fatigue, paywalls, low-effort posts.

Recover a dead post: wait 24h+, change the title to be more specific, add context, email hn@ycombinator.com if unfairly killed, or accept HN isn't the right audience.

## Build karma authentically

Long game: weeks 1-4 comment only (no submissions); months 2-3 submit interesting content that isn't yours; month 3+ occasional own work mixed with contributions. High-value comments add context from experience, share data/benchmarks, offer alternatives, give historical context.

## Show HN checklist

Live demo/download available; landing page clear; transparent pricing; creator free 2-3h; first comment drafted; title format correct; nothing posted in last 24h; account has some genuine karma.
